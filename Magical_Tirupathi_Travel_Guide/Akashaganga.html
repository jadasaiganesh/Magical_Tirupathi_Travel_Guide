Akashaganga:-

Akasha Ganga is in Tirumala . It is about 3 kms north of Srivari Temple. It is believed that Anjanadevi performed penance here and carried Anjana in her womb. It is a tradition for Tirumala Nambi clansmen to bring Akashatirtha full of three silver vessels for the Abhishekam of Lord Pratinityam. ``Akasha Ganga Theerdham'' is located about 3 km from Tirumala Srivari Temple. The Ganga that flowed in Himachal was dissolved. The Ganga that flowed in the sky and realized, this sky Ganga Martyaganga was used for the ablutions of Sri Visvesvara Swamy. The Varaha-Padma-Skanda Puranas also describe the death of Akashganga. A tirdha where a childless person is appointed as a bhokta and a sacred stone with Gardhabhamukhu is cut. The full moon day of the month of Aries with Chittanakshatra is the day for this tirdha.

History:-

The Akashaganga Temple is a significant shrine located in the sacred town of Tirumala, Andhra Pradesh, India. Here's a brief history:

Ancient Origins:

The temple's origins date back to ancient times, and it is closely associated with the rich mythology and religious history of the region. It is believed to have been established during the time when Lord Venkateswara (an incarnation of Lord Vishnu) descended to earth to bless his devotees.

Legend of Akashaganga:

According to local legends, the Akashaganga waterfall is believed to be the celestial Ganges brought down to earth by Lord Vishnu. It is said that during the time of his incarnation as Venkateswara, Lord Vishnu created this waterfall to quench the thirst of his consort, Goddess Padmavati, and to provide water to the inhabitants of Tirumala.

Significance:

The Akashaganga Temple is situated near the Akashaganga waterfall, and it holds great religious significance for devotees visiting Tirumala. Many pilgrims visit the temple to offer prayers and seek the blessings of the divine.

Pilgrimage:

The temple and the waterfall are integral parts of the pilgrimage experience for devotees visiting Tirumala. It is believed that taking a holy dip in the waters of Akashaganga is auspicious and can wash away sins.

Devotees often perform rituals and offer prayers at the temple to express their devotion to Lord Vishnu and seek his blessings for prosperity, well-being, and spiritual fulfillment. The history and significance of the Akashaganga Temple are deeply intertwined with the religious beliefs and practices of the region, making it a revered destination for pilgrims and devotees of Lord Venkateswara.

Climate around the temple:-

The climate in Tirumala, a town in the Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh, India, is predominantly tropical with distinct wet and dry seasons. Here's a general overview:

Temperature:-

Tirumala experiences hot summers and mild winters. Summer temperatures can soar, often reaching highs of 40°C (104°F) or more, while winter temperatures range from around 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F).

Rainfall:-

The town receives the majority of its rainfall during the monsoon season, which typically lasts from June to September. The southwest monsoon brings heavy rains to the region, with the peak rainfall occurring in July and August. Annual precipitation averages around 1,500 to 1,800 millimeters.

Humidity:-

Humidity levels are relatively high throughout the year due to the town's proximity to the Bay of Bengal. Humidity tends to be highest during the monsoon season and can make the heat feel more intense during the summer months.

Seasonal variations:-

The weather in Tirumala is characterized by distinct seasonal variations. Summer, from March to June, is the hottest period, with temperatures often reaching their peak. The monsoon season follows, bringing relief from the heat but also heavy rainfall. Winter, from November to February, is generally mild and pleasant.

Fog:-

Fog is not uncommon during the early mornings in the winter months, particularly in December and January. This can sometimes affect visibility, especially on the roads leading to Tirumala.

Overall, Tirumala experiences a tropical climate with hot summers, moderate rainfall during the monsoon season, and mild winters.
