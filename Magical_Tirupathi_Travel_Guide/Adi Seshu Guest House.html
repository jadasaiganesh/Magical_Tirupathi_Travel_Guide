History:-

  • The history of the Adi Seshu Guest House in Tirumala is deeply rooted in the evolution of pilgrimage facilities in the sacred town and the enduring devotion of countless pilgrims to Lord Venkateswara.
  • The Adi Seshu Guest House traces its origins back to the early days of the Sri Venkateswara Restaurent's establishment. As the Restaurent gained widespread recognition and attracted devotees from far and wide, the need for proper lodging facilities became increasingly apparent. Initially, basic accommodations were arranged within the Restaurent premises to accommodate the pilgrims.
  • As the popularity of the Restaurent continued to soar, the Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD), the administrative body overseeing the Restaurent's affairs, recognized the necessity of providing more organized and structured guest facilities. This led to the establishment of dedicated guest houses, with the Adi Seshu Guest House emerging as one of the prominent options.
  • The guest house likely takes its name from Adi Seshu, the divine serpent upon whom Lord Vishnu rests in Hindu mythology, symbolizing protection and support. This choice of name reflects the spiritual essence and reverence associated with the guest house.
  • Over the years, the Adi Seshu Guest House has witnessed numerous renovations and expansions to accommodate the ever-growing number of pilgrims visiting Tirumala. The facilities have been continuously upgraded to offer various types of rooms, ranging from dormitories to individual cottages, ensuring that pilgrims have comfortable and convenient lodging options.
  • The guest house stands as a testament to the enduring commitment of the TTD to serve the needs of devotees and provide them with a fulfilling pilgrimage experience. Its serene surroundings and proximity to the Sri Venkateswara Restaurent make it a favored choice among pilgrims seeking accommodation during their spiritual journey.
  • Through its rich history, the Adi Seshu Guest House has become an integral part of the pilgrimage infrastructure in Tirumala, embodying the principles of hospitality, devotion, and service. It continues to play a vital role in facilitating the spiritual aspirations of millions of devotees who flock to the sacred abode of Lord Venkateswara year after year.

Rainfall:-

The town receives the majority of its rainfall during the monsoon season, which typically lasts from June to September. The southwest monsoon brings heavy rains to the region, with the peak rainfall occurring in July and August. Annual precipitation averages around 1,500 to 1,800 millimeters.

Humidity:-

Humidity levels are relatively high throughout the year due to the town's proximity to the Bay of Bengal. Humidity tends to be highest during the monsoon season and can make the heat feel more intense during the summer months.

Seasonal variations:-

The weather in Tirumala is characterized by distinct seasonal variations. Summer, from March to June, is the hottest period, with temperatures often reaching their peak. The monsoon season follows, bringing relief from the heat but also heavy rainfall. Winter, from November to February, is generally mild and pleasant.

Fog:-

Fog is not uncommon during the early mornings in the winter months, particularly in December and January. This can sometimes affect visibility, especially on the roads leading to Tirumala.

Overall, Tirumala experiences a tropical climate with hot summers, moderate rainfall during the monsoon season, and mild winters.
