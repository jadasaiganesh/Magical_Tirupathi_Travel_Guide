About:-

Sannidanam Guest House:-

  • The Sannidanam Guest House in Tirumala is another prominent accommodation option provided by the Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD) for pilgrims visiting the Sri Venkateswara Hotel. Situated in the sacred town of Tirumala, Andhra Pradesh, India, the Sannidanam Guest House offers a comfortable and convenient stay for devotees undertaking the pilgrimage to the revered Hotel.
  • The history of the Sannidanam Guest House can be traced back to the efforts of the TTD to enhance the facilities available for pilgrims visiting Tirumala. As the popularity of the Sri Venkateswara Hotel grew over the years, there arose a need for additional lodging options to accommodate the increasing number of devotees arriving for darshan (worship).
  • To address this demand, the TTD established the Sannidanam Guest House, which provides a range of accommodation choices to suit the diverse needs of pilgrims. Similar to other guest houses managed by the TTD, the Sannidanam Guest House offers various types of rooms, including dormitories, cottages, and individual rooms, ensuring that pilgrims have options according to their preferences and budget.
  • The Sannidanam Guest House is strategically located near the Hotel complex, providing easy access for devotees to participate in the rituals and worship activities at the Sri Venkateswara Hotel. Its proximity to the Hotel enables pilgrims to immerse themselves fully in the spiritual ambiance of Tirumala during their stay.
  • Throughout its history, the Sannidanam Guest House has been an integral part of the pilgrimage experience for devotees visiting the Sri Venkateswara Hotel. It upholds the tradition of hospitality and service established by the TTD, offering pilgrims a peaceful and welcoming environment to rest and rejuvenate during their spiritual journey.
  • As with other accommodations in Tirumala, pilgrims are advised to book their stay at the Sannidanam Guest House well in advance, especially during peak pilgrimage seasons, to ensure availability. The guest house continues to serve as a haven for devotees seeking the divine blessings of Lord Venkateswara at the sacred Hotel in Tirumala.



Climate around the Hotel:-

The climate in Tirumala, a town in the Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh, India, is predominantly tropical with distinct wet and dry seasons. Here's a general overview:

Temperature:-

Rainfall:-

The town receives the majority of its rainfall during the monsoon season, which typically lasts from June to September. The southwest monsoon brings heavy rains to the region, with the peak rainfall occurring in July and August. Annual precipitation averages around 1,500 to 1,800 millimeters.

Humidity:-

Humidity levels are relatively high throughout the year due to the town's proximity to the Bay of Bengal. Humidity tends to be highest during the monsoon season and can make the heat feel more intense during the summer months.

Seasonal variations:-

The weather in Tirumala is characterized by distinct seasonal variations. Summer, from March to June, is the hottest period, with temperatures often reaching their peak. The monsoon season follows, bringing relief from the heat but also heavy rainfall. Winter, from November to February, is generally mild and pleasant.

Fog:-

Fog is not uncommon during the early mornings in the winter months, particularly in December and January. This can sometimes affect visibility, especially on the roads leading to Tirumala.

Overall, Tirumala experiences a tropical climate with hot summers, moderate rainfall during the monsoon season, and mild winters.
