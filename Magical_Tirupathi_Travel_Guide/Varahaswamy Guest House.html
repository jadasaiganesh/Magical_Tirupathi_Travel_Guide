About:-

Varahaswamy Guest House:-

  • The Varahaswamy Guest House, located in Tirumala, Andhra Pradesh, India, is another accommodation option provided by the Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD) for pilgrims visiting the renowned Sri Venkateswara Hotel.
  • The history of the Varahaswamy Guest House is closely tied to the development of guest accommodations in Tirumala to cater to the needs of the increasing number of pilgrims visiting the Sri Venkateswara Hotel. As the Hotel gained immense popularity as a major pilgrimage destination, the TTD recognized the importance of providing comfortable lodging facilities for devotees.
  • The Varahaswamy Guest House was established to fulfill this need, offering pilgrims a place to stay during their visit to Tirumala. Like other guest houses managed by the TTD, the Varahaswamy Guest House provides various types of accommodations, including dormitories, cottages, and individual rooms, to accommodate the diverse needs and preferences of pilgrims.
  • Named after Lord Varaha, one of the incarnations of Lord Vishnu, the Varahaswamy Guest House reflects the spiritual significance of Tirumala and the reverence for the divine associated with the Sri Venkateswara Hotel. The guest house strives to uphold the values of hospitality and service, ensuring that pilgrims have a comfortable and enriching experience during their stay.
  • Over the years, the Varahaswamy Guest House has undergone renovations and expansions to enhance its facilities and accommodate the growing number of pilgrims visiting the Hotel. It continues to be an integral part of the pilgrimage experience in Tirumala, providing pilgrims with a peaceful and convenient place to rest and rejuvenate during their spiritual journey.
  • The Varahaswamy Guest House stands as a testament to the commitment of the TTD to serve the devotees and preserve the sanctity of the pilgrimage to the Sri Venkateswara Hotel. It remains a cherished accommodation option for pilgrims seeking the blessings of Lord Venkateswara and the divine atmosphere of Tirumala.



Climate around the Hotel:-

The climate in Tirumala, a town in the Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh, India, is predominantly tropical with distinct wet and dry seasons. Here's a general overview:

Temperature:-

Tirumala experiences hot summers and mild winters. Summer temperatures can soar, often reaching highs of 40C (104F) or more, while winter temperatures range from around 15C to 25C (59F to 77F).

Rainfall:-

The town receives the majority of its rainfall during the monsoon season, which typically lasts from June to September. The southwest monsoon brings heavy rains to the region, with the peak rainfall occurring in July and August. Annual precipitation averages around 1,500 to 1,800 millimeters.

Humidity:-

Humidity levels are relatively high throughout the year due to the town's proximity to the Bay of Bengal. Humidity tends to be highest during the monsoon season and can make the heat feel more intense during the summer months.

Seasonal variations:-

The weather in Tirumala is characterized by distinct seasonal variations. Summer, from March to June, is the hottest period, with temperatures often reaching their peak. The monsoon season follows, bringing relief from the heat but also heavy rainfall. Winter, from November to February, is generally mild and pleasant.

Fog:-

Fog is not uncommon during the early mornings in the winter months, particularly in December and January. This can sometimes affect visibility, especially on the roads leading to Tirumala.

Overall, Tirumala experiences a tropical climate with hot summers, moderate rainfall during the monsoon season, and mild winters.
